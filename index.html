06 — KEY FINDINGS
Conclusions
Six Evidence-Based Findings
Each conclusion is supported directly by the data — specific numbers, not general observations.
01
Critical

Discounts above 40% produce consistent losses averaging $108 per order

1,139 orders (11.4% of total) carried a discount of 40% or more. Every one of these averaged a loss. Full-price orders averaged $67 profit. The scatter chart confirms this with loss-making orders concentrated entirely above the 40% discount line.

Delta: $175 per order between 0% and 40%+ discount
02
Loss Driver

Tables recorded $17,725 in cumulative losses — the worst sub-category in the dataset

Tables is not a low-volume product. The loss is attributable entirely to the pricing strategy applied to it. Eliminating or strictly capping discounts on Tables would have an immediate and measurable positive impact on overall margins.

03
Opportunity

Copiers generated $55,618 in profit — the highest-margin product in the catalogue

Copiers significantly outperforms all other sub-categories in net profit. It is not subject to aggressive discounting, which partly explains its margin profile. Increasing focus on Copiers represents the clearest product-level growth opportunity available.

04
Regional

West is the strongest region with $725,458 revenue and $108,418 profit

The West region leads all others in both revenue and profit. The South region generates the lowest revenue. Understanding what drives West's performance and applying those insights to underperforming regions is a scalable growth lever.

05
Category Strategy

Technology is the most profitable category at $145,455 — driven by lower average discount rates

Technology outperforms the other two categories in net profit. This margin advantage is partly explained by less aggressive discounting on Technology products. Pricing discipline — not sales volume — is the primary determinant of category-level profitability.

06
Operations

Standard Class shipping carries 59.1% of all revenue — customers consistently choose economy over speed

Standard Class dominates order volume by a significant margin. Premium shipping options represent a minority of transactions. Operational resources should be oriented toward Standard Class fulfilment efficiency rather than premium speed capabilities.

Conclusion

The data identifies a clear path to improved profitability — disciplined discounting, focus on high-margin products and replication of regional best practice.

Discount Reform

Capping discounts at 20% would eliminate most loss-making orders. High discounts destroy margin without proportional volume benefit.

Product Focus

Prioritise Copiers and Technology. Reduce or remove discounts on Tables immediately.

Regional Learning

Identify what drives West's outperformance and apply those strategies to the South region.

Seasonal Planning

Revenue peaks in Q4 annually. Inventory and capacity planning should reflect this pattern to capture peak demand.
